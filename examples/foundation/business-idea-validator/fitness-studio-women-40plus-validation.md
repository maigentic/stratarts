## Dimension 1: Problem-Solution Fit

**Score**: 8.9 / 10 (Weight: 25%)

The problem—women over 40 face muscle loss, bone density decline, metabolic changes, and intimidating fitness environments—is severe and worsening without intervention. Sarcopenia (age-related muscle loss) begins at age 30 and accelerates after 40, with women losing 3-5% of muscle mass per decade. Post-menopause, bone density loss accelerates, increasing fracture risk. These are not cosmetic concerns—they directly impact quality of life, independence, and longevity.

Problem frequency is constant and escalating. Unlike a toothbrush that needs replacement quarterly, this is an ongoing, progressive health issue. Every month without intervention, the problem worsens. This creates urgency. Women in this demographic are acutely aware of physical changes—clothes fitting differently, difficulty with activities they used to do easily, seeing parents age and lose mobility. The pain is both physical and psychological.

Current workarounds are inadequate for this specific demographic. Big-box gyms are intimidating—loud music, young crowds, complicated equipment, no guidance. Group fitness classes (Orangetheory, CrossFit, Barry's) are designed for younger, higher-fitness populations. Burpees, box jumps, and high-impact cardio are inappropriate or unsafe for women with joint issues, pelvic floor concerns, or menopause symptoms. Online coaching lacks accountability and community. General personal trainers often don't understand perimenopause/menopause physiology, hormone impacts on training, or the specific progression needs for this age group.

The proposed solution—a boutique studio with age-specific programming, menopause-aware coaching, strength focus (not cardio/weight loss), semi-private format (community + affordability), and expert guidance—directly addresses every gap. The founder's 12 years of experience with this demographic means they understand mobility limitations, injury prevention, progressive overload appropriate for older bodies, and the psychological barriers (body image, gym intimidation, comparing themselves to younger women). This is not a generic fitness offering—it's a specialized medical-adjacent service.

Willingness to pay is validated. Eight existing clients expressed intent at $300-400/month, and three committed immediately. This is strong signal—these are paying customers, not hypothetical interest. The price point is competitive with boutique studios ($200-300/month for unlimited classes) but offers far more personalization. It's more expensive than a big-box gym ($40-80/month) but addresses needs those gyms don't meet. The target demographic has disposable income and prioritizes health—this is a high-value purchase.

**Key Insight**: The problem is severe, frequent, and progressively worsening—creating strong urgency—and existing solutions fail to address the specific needs of this demographic. Your specialized solution directly fills a genuine market gap.

**Evidence**:

- **Objective**: Identify viable locations and understand real estate costs

- **Cost**: $0 (time only, broker fees paid by landlord)
